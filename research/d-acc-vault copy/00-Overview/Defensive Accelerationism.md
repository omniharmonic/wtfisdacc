# Defensive Accelerationism (D/ACC)

Defensive Accelerationism represents a philosophical and investment framework that embraces technological progress while prioritizing technologies that strengthen defense, decentralization, and democratic participation over those that concentrate power or enable harm.

## Core Philosophy

D/ACC emerges from the recognition that technological acceleration is inevitable, but its direction is not. The framework asks: *how do we ensure that progress benefits humanity broadly rather than concentrating power in the hands of a few?*

The answer lies in the **offense/defense balance**. D/ACC favors technologies where defense is structurally advantaged—where the cost of causing harm exceeds the cost of preventing it, and where power naturally distributes rather than concentrates.

## The Four D's Framework

Every technology is evaluated against four criteria:

1. **[[Defensive]]** - Does it shift the offense/defense balance toward protection?
2. **[[Decentralized]]** - Can it operate without a single controlling entity?
3. **[[Democratic]]** - Does it enable broader participation in decisions?
4. **[[Differential]]** - Does defense capability grow faster than offense?

See [[Four Ds Framework]] for the complete evaluation methodology.

## Defense Categories

D/ACC organizes defensive technologies into four domains:

### [[Macro Physical Defense]]
Resilient infrastructure, decentralized connectivity, independent energy, multi-planetary civilization. Technologies enabling people to survive independently of long international supply chains.

### [[Micro Physical Defense]]
Far-UVC irradiation, air filtering, rapid vaccine platforms, wastewater surveillance. Public health infrastructure woven into civilization without social compulsion.

### [[Cyber Defense]]
Blockchains, [[Zero-Knowledge Proofs]], cryptocurrencies, [[Social Recovery Wallets]], trusted hardware. Economic and social structures with shared infrastructure without centralized dependency.

### [[Info Defense]]
Community Notes-style fact-checking, [[Prediction Markets]], consensus-finding algorithms, wallet-level scam detection. Avoiding pre-assumed authorities that "know what is true and false."

## Investment Framework
