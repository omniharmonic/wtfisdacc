# d/acc Investment Framework One-Pager

**Defensive Accelerationism as Investment Strategy**

---

## The Core Insight

Technology is powerful and accelerating. The key question isn't whether to build, but **what** to build. d/acc focuses on technologies that favor **defense over offense** — making it harder to attack and easier to protect.

> "A defense-favoring world is a better world... it makes it easier for healthier, more open and more freedom-respecting forms of governance to thrive." — Vitalik Buterin

## The Four D's Filter

Every d/acc investment must satisfy:

| Filter | Question |
|--------|----------|
| **Defensive** | Does it shift the offense/defense balance toward defense? |
| **Decentralized** | Is control distributed across many actors? |
| **Democratic** | Does it empower individuals over institutions? |
| **Differential** | Does it favor good actors over bad actors? |

## Red Flags (Automatic Downgrade)

- ⚠️ Single point of failure in key infrastructure
- ⚠️ Centralized control of upgrade mechanism
- ⚠️ Closed source core components
- ⚠️ Token distribution favoring insiders >50%
- ⚠️ History of censorship or deplatforming

---

## Green Flags (Bonus Consideration)

- ✅ Government-independent operation demonstrated
- ✅ Survived adversarial conditions (sanctions, attacks)
- ✅ Vitalik direct endorsement or Balvi/EF funding
- ✅ Community Notes-style bridging algorithm
- ✅ Formal verification of core contracts

---

## The Counterfactual Test

Before investing, ask: **"What happens if this project doesn't exist?"**

**Strong alignment**: Users have no privacy-preserving alternative, coordination becomes centralized, defense capability degraded.

**Weak alignment**: Multiple alternatives exist, marginal improvement, primarily financial optimization.
