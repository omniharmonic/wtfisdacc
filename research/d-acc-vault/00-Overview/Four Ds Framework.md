# The Four D's Framework

The Four D's Framework provides a systematic methodology for evaluating technology alignment with [[Defensive Accelerationism]] principles. Each criterion carries equal weight (25%) in the overall assessment.

## The Four Criteria

### Defensive

> **Core Question**: Does this make it easier to defend against harm than to cause harm?

Technologies that shift the offense/defense balance toward protection rather than attack.

**Red Flags:**
- Creates new attack surfaces
- Enables cheaper harm than protection
- Facilitates surveillance infrastructure

**Example Pass**: [[Zero-Knowledge Proofs]] enabling verification without exposure; [[Social Recovery Wallets]] protecting against key loss

**Example Fail**: Drone swarms that could be weaponized; Surveillance AI that centralizes monitoring power

---

### Decentralized

> **Core Question**: Can this operate without requiring trust that centralized actors will remain benevolent?

Technologies that can function without a single controlling entity or trusted third party.

**Red Flags:**
- Requires trust in "good guys" staying good
- Single points of failure
- Centralized upgrade authority

**Example Pass**: Bitcoin's permissionless consensus; IPFS content addressing

**Example Fail**: Trusted bridge multisigs; Centralized oracle providers

---

### Democratic

> **Core Question**: Does this enable more people to participate meaningfully in governance and coordination?

Technologies that enable broader participation in decisions and resist capture by small groups.

**Red Flags:**
- Concentrates capability in small groups
- Creates plutocratic dynamics
- Excludes participation based on resources

**Example Pass**: [[Quadratic Funding]] reducing whale dominance; Community Notes finding cross-partisan consensus

**Example Fail**: Token-weighted voting with whale concentration; Closed governance councils

---

### Differential

> **Core Question**: Does this make defense grow faster than offense over time?

Technologies that accelerate defense capabilities faster than offense capabilities.

**Red Flags:**
- Equally useful for harmful applications
- Offense benefits more from improvements
- Creates arms race dynamics

**Example Pass**: Far-UVC (kills pathogens universally without targeting); Air filtering (pure defense)

**Example Fail**: Autonomous weapons (offense/defense neutral); General-purpose drones

## Scoring Methodology

| Score | Alignment | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 5 | Perfect | Passes all Four D's; shifts balance decisively toward defense |
| 4 | Excellent | Passes most criteria; clear defensive value |
| 3 | Moderate | Passes some criteria; context-dependent |
| 2 | Weak | Concentration concerns; limited defensive value |
| 1 | Poor | Fails most criteria; potential for harm |

## Application to Investment

Technologies scoring 5 → [[Tier 1 - Highest Conviction]]
Technologies scoring 4 → [[Tier 2 - Strong Conviction]]
Technologies scoring 3 or below → [[Tier 3 - Selective Conviction]]
