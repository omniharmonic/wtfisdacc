# Adversarial Scenarios Overview

Threat modeling for D/ACC-aligned systems. Understanding how adversaries might attack defensive infrastructure helps identify vulnerabilities and inform investment priorities.

## Adversary Types

| Type | Motivation | Resources |
|------|------------|-----------|
| **Nation-State** | Control, surveillance, sanctions | Unlimited legal + technical |
| **Corporate/Market** | Profit, competitive advantage | Significant financial |
| **Regulatory** | Compliance, control | Legal authority |
| **Criminal** | Theft, extortion | Technical sophistication |
| **Technical** | Discovery, exploit | Knowledge, compute |

## Scenario Categories by Impact

### Catastrophic (System-Ending)

- [[Zero-Day in Cryptographic Primitive]] - Fundamental flaw breaks all dependent systems
- [[Quantum Computing Breakthrough]] - Existing cryptography compromised

### Critical (Major Disruption)

- [[Nation-State Validator Coercion]] - Censorship at consensus level
- [[Coordinated Exchange Attack]] - Market manipulation
- [[Supply Chain Compromise]] - Backdoored dependencies
- [[Economic Attack on Consensus]] - 51% attacks
- [[Insider Attack on Protocol Team]] - Privileged access abuse

### High (Significant Damage)

- [[Regulatory Capture of Bridges]] - Cross-chain chokepoint control
- [[Governance Capture via Token Accumulation]] - Protocol direction shift
- [[Targeted Developer Persecution]] - Key person removal
- [[Coordinated Deplatforming]] - Multi-provider shutoff
- [[Smart Contract Bug Exploitation]] - Logic errors exploited

### Medium (Localized Impact)

- [[Sophisticated Phishing at Scale]] - AI-powered social engineering
- [[Sybil Attack on Identity Systems]] - Fake identity creation
