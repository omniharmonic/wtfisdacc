## Overview

Price-stable digital assets enabling reliable value transfer and DeFi participation. The market has grown to $309B market cap with $27.6T annual volume—exceeding Visa+Mastercard combined. However, 91% remains centralized.

## Why D/ACC Concern

The 91% centralization creates concentration risk and surveillance capability. USDT (60%) and USDC (20%) can freeze addresses, comply with sanctions, and are subject to regulatory capture.

## Why Decentralized Stablecoins Are D/ACC Aligned

Decentralized alternatives (DAI, Ethena) preserve utility while maintaining decentralization—reducing single points of failure in monetary infrastructure.

## Investment Thesis

**[[Decentralized Stablecoin Thesis]]**

$309B market cap with regulatory pressure on centralized stablecoins creating opportunity. Ethena grew from $146M to $6.2B demonstrating demand for alternatives.

**Risk factors:**
- Historical peg stability failures (algorithmic designs)
- Regulatory uncertainty
- Centralized stablecoin network effects

## Counterfactual Analysis

**What if decentralized stablecoins fail to scale?**
- Centralized stablecoins dominate entirely
- Regulatory capture complete
- **Vacuum filled by**: CBDCs, fully surveilled payment rails, bank-issued stablecoins
- **Impact**: Strongly Negative - monetary infrastructure becomes surveillance tool
