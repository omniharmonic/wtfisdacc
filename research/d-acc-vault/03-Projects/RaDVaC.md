# RaDVaC (Rapid Deployment Vaccine Collaborative)

**Category**: Biosecurity > Open Source Vaccines
**D/ACC Domain**: Biosecurity & Biodefense
**Quadrant**: Atoms-Survive
**Stage**: Active | **Funding**: $2.5M Balvi Grant

## Overview

RaDVaC is a decentralized scientific collaboration developing open-source vaccine designs and production methods. Their goal is to establish "vaccine factory in a tube" technology that anyone can use, bridging the vaccine access gap globally.

## Why Open Source Vaccines?

Traditional vaccine development:
- Takes 10-15 years
- Costs $1B+
- Creates access gaps between rich and poor countries
- Controlled by pharmaceutical companies

RaDVaC's approach:
- Open-source designs anyone can produce
- Rapid iteration and updates
- Decentralized production capability
- Citizen-science right to vaccine creation

## Balvi Grant ($2.5M)

The grant supports:
- Continued development and testing
- "Vaccine factory in a tube" technology
- Build-out of decentralized scientific network
- Open sharing of production methods
- Clinical trial and immunology data sharing

## Shared Values with D/ACC

Both RaDVaC and Balvi are committed to:
- Bridging the vaccine access gap
- Establishing citizen-science right to vaccine creation
- Vaccine access as a public good
- Fostering decentralized, open-source research
