# Pol.is

**Category**: Information Resilience > Consensus-Finding
**D/ACC Domain**: Social Technology
**Quadrant**: Bits-Thrive
**Stage**: Deployed | **License**: Open Source

## Overview

Pol.is is a real-time survey system that finds consensus among large groups. Unlike traditional social media that optimizes for engagement (and division), Pol.is surfaces areas of agreement and hides divisive statements, provocation, and trolling.

## How It Works

1. Users write comments OR agree/disagree/pass on others' comments
2. **Critically: Users cannot reply to each other** (prevents flame wars)
3. Algorithm clusters users by voting patterns
4. Surfaces statements that achieve cross-group consensus
5. Gamifies finding consensus—more agreement = more visibility

## vTaiwan: National-Scale Success

Taiwan's vTaiwan is the longest-running national implementation of Pol.is:
- **80%+ of deliberations led to government action**
- Used for 12+ pieces of legislation
- Famous Uber case: 4000+ participants, 4 weeks, unanimous resolution
- Also: FinTech sandbox act, revenge porn regulation

Audrey Tang (former Taiwan Digital Minister): "People spend far more time discovering their commonalities rather than going down a rabbit hole on a particular issue. Invariably, within three weeks or four, we always find a shape where most people agree on most of the statements."

## Global Impact

- Influenced Twitter's Community Notes design (2022)
- Used in USA, Canada, Singapore, Philippines, Finland, Spain
- Anthropic explored using Pol.is for AI model steering (2023)
